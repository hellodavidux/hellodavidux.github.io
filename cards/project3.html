EASY SEMESTER PROJECT

I co-founded and launched Easy Semester, a circular rental service providing exchange students in Denmark with affordable, sustainable bedding packs, streamlining their arrival and reducing waste.

Year

2019

Company

Easy Semester IVS

Product

Linen rental service

My Role

Co-founder

Team

Three co-founders with student volunteers

Project context

Exchange students in Denmark often arrive without basic household items and spend significant time and money buying new linens, only to discard them at the end of their stay.

This creates unnecessary waste and inconvenience, putting a financial and logistical burden on students during an already stressful transition period.

Goal

  • Make student arrival easier and more affordable
  • Create an environmentally friendly solution
  • Minimize waste through circular economy

DISCOVERY

Research Question

How might we minimize waste and make it easy and affordable for exchange students to get essential household items when arriving in Denmark?

Users

Exchange Students

We conducted surveys with exchange students to identify their biggest pain points and needs regarding accommodation essentials, confirming high demand for affordable, sustainable, and convenient solutions.

1. From sleeping on the floor to circular solutions

During my first week as an international student in Denmark, I had to sleep without basic bedding, which inspired me and my friends to create a service that would ensure no student had to face the same challenge.

2. Understanding student needs before Launch

We surveyed exchange students to identify their biggest pain points and needs regarding accommodation essentials, confirming high demand for affordable, sustainable, and convenient solutions.

3. Mapping the exchange student Journey

Using an empathy map, we visualized students' jobs, pains, and gains, highlighting their need for cost-effective, sustainable solutions and the stress caused by sourcing and disposing of household goods.

MVP PROCESS

1. Designing a Circular, Student-Centric Service

We used the Business Model Canvas to map out our value proposition, key partners, activities, resources, channels, customer relationships, cost structure, and revenue streams, ensuring a holistic and scalable solution.

2. The solution

The plan was to reuse material from leaving exchange students, and sell it to incoming students. We reached out to our university housing office, but they didn't want to collaborate, as they were selling their own new linen packs.

3. We made a landing page and sent 800 emails to exchange students

A simple e-commerce site allowed students to reserve linen packs before arrival. We sent 800 emails to exchange students promoting our service. We got the email addresses by figuring out the university email structure.

4. We had a busy summer, collecting, cleaning and delivering linen packs

We launched a minimum viable product by collecting donated linens, preparing rental packs, building a basic website for orders, and managing logistics and deliveries ourselves, primarily by bike and GreenMobility

INSIGHTS

Circular Economy Impact
The service was well-received: 74 students donated materials, 1,250 students from 40+ nationalities visited the site, and 60 orders were completed in two semesters.
Feedback and future plans
We received positive feedback in surveys and casual chats. We were soexcited that were planning to increase our offering to furniture, other items and even create student community events.
COVID-19 Impact
The pandemic effectively halted our operations as international exchange programs were suspended for over a year, resulting in no new students arriving to study in Denmark.
